About Me

Hello! I'm Rishideep, a postgraduate student in Advanced Computing at Imperial College London. I recently graduated from RCC Institute of Information Technology with a Bachelor of Technology (B.Tech) in Computer Science & Engineering.

My fascination with software systems was sparked during my early adolescent years, when I attempted to build an application that could switch users across multiple devices. Little did I know that this digital venture would become my gateway to comprehending the intricate dance of computers.

Conference Presentation & Publication

Automated Landslide Event Archive Generation from News Articles

Rishideep Chatterjee, Subhayan Bhattacharya, Dr. Sarbani Roy

IEEE ASIANCON 2023 • [PDF] • Acknowledgement

S. Bhattacharya, R. Chatterjee and S. Roy, "Automated Landslide Event Archive Generation from News Articles," 2023 3rd Asian Conference on Innovation in Technology (ASIANCON), Ravet IN, India, 2023, pp. 1-8, doi: 10.1109/ASIANCON58793.2023.10270576.

Key Highlights
  • Proposed a novel framework for generating landslide archive from online news articles
  • BERT QA model configured with questions about date, location, and number of people affected
  • Manually annotated 800+ news articles into classes High, Low, and Uncertain
  • Compared six classifiers: Multi Layer Perceptron, Decision Tree, Random Forest, Support Vector Classifier, K Nearest Neighbour, and Stochastic Gradient Descent
  • Stochastic Gradient Descent (SGD) classifier achieved the most promising results
  • Developed an end-to-end framework with user-friendly web interface for querying based on time and location

Projects & Unpublished Work

Noise Resistant Local Binary Pattern Descriptor - A Supervised Approach

Undergraduate Research Thesis • [PDF]

  • A powerful tool for texture recognition, Local Binary Pattern (LBP) descriptor is often sensitive to image noise, compromising visual quality & recognition accuracy
  • The project presents a noise-resistant LBP descriptor for robust texture recognition through image processing - the approach involves four stages:
    (1) image preprocessing,
    (2) LBP feature extraction,
    (3) noise-resistant thresholding, and
    (4) texture pattern classification using a Machine Learning algorithm
  • Experimental results on the Brodatz texture dataset and the OASIS Brain MRI dataset demonstrate superior performance
  • Tech Stack: Python CV2, Scikit-learn, Numpy
